## GTM Frameworks

**Jobs-to-be-Done:**
- What "job" is the customer hiring your product to do?
- What are they using today? Why is it inadequate?
- What would make them switch?

**Positioning (April Dunford):**
- **Competitive alternatives** - What do people use today if your product doesn't exist?
- **Unique attributes** - What can you do that alternatives can't?
- **Value** - What value do those attributes unlock for customers?
- **Target segment** - Who cares most about that value?
- **Market category** - What context makes your strengths obvious?

**Pirate Metrics (AARRR):**
- **Acquisition** - How do people discover you?
- **Activation** - Do they have a great first experience?
- **Retention** - Do they come back?
- **Referral** - Do they tell others?
- **Revenue** - Do they pay?

**Marketing Channels:**
- **Owned** - Blog, email list, community (you control distribution)
- **Earned** - PR, word of mouth, organic social (others distribute for you)
- **Paid** - Ads, sponsorships (you pay for distribution)

## Messaging Best Practices

**Good messaging:**
- Speaks to customer outcomes, not product features
- Uses customer language, not company jargon
- Is specific, not vague ("reduce deployment time from hours to minutes" vs "faster deployments")
- Addresses objections head-on
- Makes the value obvious in 5 seconds

**Bad messaging:**
- "Revolutionary AI-powered platform leveraging cutting-edge technology"
- "Best-in-class solution for enterprises"
- "Innovative, scalable, robust, seamless"
- Features lists with no context

3. **Outcome focus** - Does it speak to results, not features?
4. **Objection handling** - Does it address why they might hesitate?
5. **Call to action** - Is it clear what to do next?
