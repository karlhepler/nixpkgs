## Key Financial Concepts

**Unit Economics:**
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** - Sales + marketing spend / new customers
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)** - Average revenue per customer × gross margin × lifetime
- **LTV:CAC Ratio** - Should be 3:1 or better for healthy SaaS
- **Payback Period** - CAC / (MRR × gross margin) - target <12 months
- **Contribution Margin** - Revenue - variable costs (%)

**SaaS Metrics:**
- **MRR/ARR** - Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue
- **Net Revenue Retention** - Expansion - contraction - churn (target >100%)
- **Gross Churn** - Lost MRR / beginning MRR (target <2% monthly)
- **Magic Number** - Net new ARR / prior quarter S&M spend (target >0.75)
- **Rule of 40** - Growth rate + profit margin should be >40%

**Cash Flow:**
- **Burn Rate** - Cash out per month
- **Runway** - Cash in bank / burn rate (months left)
- **Cash Conversion** - How quickly revenue turns into cash
- **Working Capital** - Current assets - current liabilities

**Fundraising:**
- **Pre/Post Money Valuation** - Company value before/after investment
- **Dilution** - Investment / post-money valuation (%)
- **Cap Table** - Who owns what percentage
- **Liquidation Preference** - Who gets paid first in exit (1x is standard)

## Pricing Strategy

**Value-Based Pricing:**
- Price based on value delivered, not cost incurred
- Anchor to customer outcomes ("this saves you $X")
- Willingness to pay varies by segment - price accordingly

**Packaging:**
- Good/Better/Best (3 tiers is optimal)
- Middle tier should be the obvious choice (anchor effect)
- Top tier exists to make middle tier look reasonable

**Common Pricing Mistakes:**
- Pricing too low (leaves money on table, signals low value)
- Too many tiers (decision paralysis)
- Feature-based differentiation without value differentiation
- Not raising prices as value increases

## Financial Modeling Best Practices

**Model Structure:**
- **Drivers** - Customer metrics, pricing, costs (the levers you control)
- **Revenue** - Built from drivers (customers × price × usage)
- **Costs** - Fixed vs variable, by category
- **Cash Flow** - Revenue - costs - capex + financing

**Assumptions:**
- Document every assumption
- Sensitivity analysis on key assumptions
- Conservative is better than optimistic
- Validate assumptions against market data

**Scenarios:**
- Base case (most likely)
- Upside (things go well)
- Downside (things go poorly)
